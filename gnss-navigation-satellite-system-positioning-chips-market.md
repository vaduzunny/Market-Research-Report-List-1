<p><p>The GNSS Positioning Chips market is divided into two main types: high-precision and standard-precision GNSS positioning chips. High-precision GNSS positioning chips offer centimeter-level accuracy, making them ideal for applications such as surveying, autonomous vehicles, and precision agriculture. In contrast, standard-precision GNSS positioning chips provide meter-level accuracy and are commonly used in consumer devices like smartphones, wearables, and basic navigation systems. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing location-based services, but cater to different accuracy needs and application requirements.</p></p>
