Molecular diagnostics utilize techniques like PCR for rapid genetic identification. Differential light scattering analyzes particle characteristics for pathogen detection. Immunoassays employ antigen-antibody interactions for quantifying E. coli levels. Flow cytometry offers cell counting and analysis, while chromatography separates biological molecules for testing. Finally, diagnostic imaging aids in visualizing infections, enhancing overall detection capabilities in clinical settings.</p></p>
